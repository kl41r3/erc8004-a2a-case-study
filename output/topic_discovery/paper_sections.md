\subsubsection{BERTopic: embedding-based divergence}
We embed all 4,372 records (ERC-8004: 142; A2A: 4,230) with the \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} sentence transformer~\cite{reimers_gurevych_2019}, reduce to five dimensions with UMAP~\cite{mcinnes_umap_2018} (cosine metric, \texttt{n\_neighbors}=15), cluster with HDBSCAN (\texttt{min\_cluster\_size}=10), and extract topic representations via BERTopic's class-based TF-IDF~\cite{bertopic_2022}. Cross-case discourse separation is quantified with the Jensen--Shannon divergence~\cite{lin_1991} between the two cases' empirical topic distributions.

BERTopic identifies 19 topics plus a noise class over the combined corpus. The global Jensen--Shannon divergence between the two cases' topic distributions is $\mathrm{JSD}_{\text{BERTopic}}=0.288$, a moderate but meaningful structural separation (Figure~\ref{fig:bertopic-divergence}). ERC-8004 is strikingly concentrated: 67.6\% of its records fall into Topic~0 (\textit{agent, agents, for, of}), the broad agent-discourse cluster, versus 21.2\% for A2A. Implementation-heavy topics---Task/Message management (8.2\%), JSON/proto spec (7.7\%), PR-contribution workflows (7.2\%), SDK samples (5.9\%)---carry substantial A2A weight but zero ERC-8004 records, confirming that low-level engineering is absent from the EIP forum. The only topic with higher ERC share than A2A is Topic~9 (\textit{suggestion, sap, linkedin}; 2.8\% vs.\ 0.9\%), signalling corporate voices (SAP, LinkedIn) entering the EIP thread.

\subsubsection{CryptoBERT: domain-adapted embedding validation}
To assess whether ERC-8004's topic concentration is an artefact of a general-purpose embedding model rather than a genuine structural property, we re-embed the 142 ERC-8004 records with CryptoBERT (\texttt{ElKulako/cryptobert}), a RoBERTa-based model further pre-trained on 3.2~million cryptocurrency social-media posts (StockTwits, Reddit, Telegram, Twitter)~\cite{kulakowski2023}. Mean-pooled hidden states (768~dimensions, 128-token truncation) replace all-MiniLM-L6-v2 as the embedding backbone; UMAP and HDBSCAN parameters are identical to Method~2 except that \texttt{min\_cluster\_size} is reduced from 10 to 5 to accommodate the smaller corpus.

CryptoBERT yields five coherent topics with 6.3\% noise, and the dominant structure mirrors Method~2 closely. Two topics jointly account for 73.2\% of records: Topic~0 (\textit{agents, agent, onchain, reputation, registry}; 53.5\%) and Topic~2 (\textit{agent, trust, feedback, reputation, agent~card}; 19.7\%). The crypto-aware encoder thus replicates the agent-discourse concentration found by all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (67.6\% in Topic~0), confirming robustness to embedding choice. Beyond replication, the domain-adapted model resolves a distinction invisible to the general encoder: Topic~0 foregrounds the on-chain infrastructure layer (\textit{onchain, registry, reputation}), while Topic~2 foregrounds the trust-negotiation layer (\textit{trust, feedback, agent~card}). The remaining topics map onto governance-process activity---Topic~4 (\textit{merge, uint256, github, pr}; 9.2\%) captures EIP lifecycle events, where \textit{uint256} (a Solidity integer type) signals that EIP reviewers evaluate implementation-level implications even at the specification stage; Topic~3 (\textit{reviewers, marcometamask, abcoathup}; 6.3\%) surfaces named institutional participants; and Topic~1 (\textit{implementation, protocol, lightweight, discover}; 4.9\%) covers technical specification discourse.

Taken together, the CryptoBERT analysis strengthens the cross-method convergence claim: regardless of whether embeddings encode general semantic similarity or crypto-domain lexical patterns, ERC-8004 discourse concentrates on agent-trust and on-chain reputation mechanisms rather than implementation detail. The domain-adapted embeddings add granularity by separating infrastructure concerns from trust-negotiation concerns, two aspects that the general model collapsed into a single broad cluster.

\paragraph{Limitation.} CryptoBERT was pre-trained on short social-media posts (128-token maximum); EIP forum posts average longer and more structured prose. Texts exceeding 128 tokens are truncated, potentially discarding late-occurring technical arguments. Future work could explore a crypto-domain sentence-transformer trained on longer governance texts.
